<p><p>The polyethylene glycol (PEG) market is categorized into two primary types: Type I and Type II. Type I includes low molecular weight PEGs, typically used in pharmaceuticals and cosmetics for their solubilizing and moisturizing properties. Type II consists of higher molecular weight PEGs, often utilized in industrial applications such as lubricants, adhesives, and coatings due to their thickening and film-forming characteristics. Both types cater to distinct sectors, addressing specific functional requirements in various applications.</p></p>
